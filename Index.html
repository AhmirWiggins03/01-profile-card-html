Intel Sustainability Goals Timeline

Explore Intel’s journey toward a sustainable future. Scroll through our timeline to see how we’re innovating for the planet with bold goals and real progress.

About Intel: Founded in 1968, Intel has been at the forefront of semiconductor innovation, powering the digital age with its microprocessors and technologies. From the first 4004 chip to today’s AI and edge computing, Intel’s legacy is built on relentless innovation, responsibility, and a vision for a smarter, more sustainable world.

Looking Ahead: Intel is committed to advancing not only technology but also environmental and social progress. With ambitious sustainability goals, investments in green manufacturing, and leadership in responsible computing, Intel aims to enable a positive impact for generations to come.
Intel Founded
1968
Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore establish Intel in California.
Intel’s founding marked the start of the modern semiconductor industry. The company’s name comes from “Integrated Electronics.”
First Microprocessor: 4004
1971
Intel launches the world’s first commercial microprocessor.
The 4004 was a 4-bit CPU that revolutionized computing, enabling the development of smaller, more powerful devices.
Pentium Processor
1993
The Pentium brand debuts, powering a new era of PCs.
Pentium processors brought advanced performance and multimedia capabilities to mainstream computers worldwide.
Core i7 Launch
2008
Intel introduces the Core i7, redefining high-performance computing.
The Core i7 series set new standards for speed, efficiency, and multitasking in desktops and laptops.
100% Green Power
2022
Intel achieves 100% renewable electricity use in U.S., Europe, Israel, and Malaysia.
By investing in solar, wind, and other renewables, Intel powers its major global operations with clean energy, reducing its carbon footprint and setting a standard for the tech industry.
Net Positive Water
2025 Goal
Return more freshwater to communities than Intel withdraws.
Intel restores water through conservation and community projects, aiming to replenish 100% of its global water use and support local ecosystems.
Zero Waste to Landfill
2030 Goal
Achieve zero hazardous waste to landfill across all operations.
Through recycling, reuse, and innovative waste management, Intel is committed to sending zero hazardous waste to landfills by 2030.
Net Zero Greenhouse Gas Emissions
2040 Goal
Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions for global operations.
Intel is investing in energy efficiency, green buildings, and carbon offsets to reach net-zero emissions by 2040, leading the way for a climate-resilient future.
Circular Economy Leadership
Ongoing
Designing products and processes for reuse, recycling, and minimal waste.
Intel advances a circular economy by designing chips and packaging for longevity, recyclability, and minimal environmental impact.
AI & Future Innovation
2020s & Beyond
Intel leads in AI, edge computing, and sustainable technology.
Intel is shaping the future with breakthroughs in artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and responsible manufacturing—enabling a smarter, greener, and more connected world.
